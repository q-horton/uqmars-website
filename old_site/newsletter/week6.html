  • Microcontroller Workshop

    Location: 46-243 - Andrew N. Liveris Building
    Time: Tuesday 28 March, 6pm
    Type: Technical Event
    Difficulty: Beginner
    Are you interested in learning how to control robots with microcontrollers? Do you want to spice up your ENGG1100 project with wireless communication? Then join us for the UQ MARS microcontroller workshop in week 6!

    In this workshop, you will learn:

    - What a microcontroller is and what types of microcontrollers are out there
    - How to program in the Arduino IDE and upload code to your microcontroller
    - How to use PWM to control a servo motor
    - How to use an ESP32 to communicate wirelessly with another device

    This workshop is designed for beginners who have little or no experience with microcontrollers. It is also ideal for first-year engineers who are doing ENGG1100 and want to learn some useful skills for their robot design project. Wireless control can give you an edge over your competitors!
    UQ MARS will provide ESP32s and some Arduino Unos for the workshop, but you are encouraged to bring your own microcontroller if you have one. You will also need a laptop with the Arduino IDE installed.

    The workshop will be held on 28th March at 6pm in 46-243 - Andrew N. Liveris Building, Collaborative Room.

    The microcontroller workshop is part of a series of workshops that UQ MARS is running this semester that are relevant for ENGG1100 students. So, keep an eye out for future workshops we have planned.
