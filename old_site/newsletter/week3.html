  • Robots in Human Spaces

    Location: Hawken 50-N202
    Time: Tuesday 14 March at 6pm
    Type: Technical Event
    Difficulty: Moderate
    Join us in week 4 for a great talk by ITEE Associate Professor in Mechatronics Dr Jen Jen Chung!
    Everyone will have the chance to ask Dr Chung questions and enjoy some free pizza afterwards! üçïü§ñ

    Abstract:

    The tremendous potential offered by robotic automation has been evidenced by its revolution of large-scale manufacturing, mining, and agriculture.

    Now, with increasing social and economic pressures to transfer this success to service-focused sectors, we're seeing robots moving into our homes, onto our roads and into our airspace. These human-centred spaces heighten the challenges of robotic perception for scene understanding and emphasise the need for robust planning and interaction in unstructured and dynamic environments.

    This talk will focus on how we are tackling these challenges in robotic mobile manipulation scenarios, i.e., tasks where the robot needs to both navigate collision-free as well as physically grasp and manipulate objects within its environment.

    We will discuss the importance of problem representation and how this impacts the system's ability to reason and adapt under uncertainty. Finally, we will take an outlook on how these techniques can be further developed to achieve robots that not only operate in our spaces but that are also capable of rich and intuitive collaboration with people.

Weekly Member Shout-Out

  • Francisco Jorquera Prado (CISCO)


    I always have to be doing something. I'm the type of guy who wants to be a part of something bigger than myself. I also enjoy learning new concepts (even if said concept may make the rail way tracks look extra comfy).

    My peers and myself are a part of UQ MARS' Droid Race Challenge (DRC) team and we plan to take home gold. Our team comprises of mostly electrical engineers, however each is specialised in a way, shape or form. Myself for example, I try to be as diverse as possible when it comes to my abilities but my strengths mainly lie in chassis design and python programming. Currently my friends and I have created a program that takes input from a Xbox One controller wirelessly and have the onboard computer (running Ubuntu) communicate with the arduino to drive around.


    Hardly autonomous at the moment, but a great start. As of right now the bot is called BEBOOP and is effectively an RC car, with a computer on it. Next we are working on the computer vision and camera drivers so that the robot can see.
