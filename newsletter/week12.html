MARS Newsletter - Week 12

Hey UQ MARS crew,

Hope you're all doing well and are pumped for some awesome events we've got coming up! First on the agenda, we're teaming up with SMACK and UQFPS for a movie night on Tuesday, 16th May. We'll be diving into the sci-fi classic, "Ghost in the Shell". It's a perfect opportunity to chill, connect with your mates, and enjoy some top-notch tech cinema. So, jot down the date, 5:30 pm at Advanced Engineering (49) Room 200.

Then on Tuesday, 23rd May, we're hosting a Project Showcase Night with RobotX, UQRacing, and UQSpace. You'll get to see some truly innovative projects that are sure to inspire. The fun begins at 6pm in the Andrew N. Liveris Building, Collaborative Room.

And hey, how are our Droid Race Challenge teams going? Shoutout to our UQ MARS DRC Teams 1, 2, 3, and 4 who have started working on their droids. A big high five to Team 3, who are currently nailing it! We're super excited by the progress and can't wait to see what the other teams bring to the table. Remember, a little friendly rivalry never hurt anyone and is the perfect fuel for innovation.

We're looking forward to hanging out with you at our events and are super keen to see your droid designs. Keep up the awesome work.

Catch you tonight at 49-200,
The UQ MARS Team

Weekly Member Shout-Out

  • Quinn Horton





    G'Day all!

    I'm Quinn, a third year BE/ME student under the mechatronics field of study, and the UQ MARS treasurer for 2023.

    My areas of interest lie heavily within the control engineering / embedded systems side of things, or generally being able to program things, especially when I get to see something physically move.



    Alongside my studies and MARS, I'm also an EAIT faculty ambassador, a Youth Outreach Coordinator with the UQ Chapter of Engineers Without Borders, and one of the course tutors for ENGG1100.

    Projects-wise, I haven't got a lot to show off in the robotics space (I'd like to thank METR2800 for scaring me away from it a little), but I will be competing in the Roborave competition with MARS in July.

    Beyond engineering projects, I also have many things that I like to work on with my roles in student societies, including revitalising the MARS infrastructure, working on our mechatronics course guide, the recent Arduino Hackathon, the Games Night with UQ Mafia and Tabletop Wargaming, and plenty more to role out throughout the rest of this year and beyond.
